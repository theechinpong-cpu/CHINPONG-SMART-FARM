In the **Writing Skill** section, we focus on advanced grammatical structures. The **Future Perfect Tense** (will have + past participle) is the primary tool for making specific predictions about future completions. It is often used with "By" or "By the time" to indicate that an action will be finished before another point in the future [cite: 2026-02-16]. Furthermore, the exam evaluates your mastery of **Conditionals**. **Type 2 Conditionals** (If + Past Simple, would + infinitive) allow us to discuss hypothetical, imaginary present situations. **Type 3 Conditionals** (If + Past Perfect, would have + past participle) are used to express regrets or speculate about alternative past outcomes [cite: 2026-02-16].

Lastly, **Relative Clauses** (using who, which, whose, that, where) are essential for academic writing. They enable you to define and describe people, objects, and places with precision, making your sentences more cohesive and sophisticated. The **Reading** section will challenge your ability to extract specific information from texts about smart technology and environmental challenges, requiring a synthesis of all these linguistic skills.

💡 Memory Tricks & Thought Process:
- **The "By" Rule:** If a sentence starts with "By 2050" or "By next week," your brain should immediately search for "will have + V.3" [cite: 2026-02-16].

- **Whose vs. Who:** Use 'Whose' only when something belongs to someone (The boy whose bag...).
