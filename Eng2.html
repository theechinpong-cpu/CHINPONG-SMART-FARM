Unit 4: Customs & Traditions

1. Subject Matter (English)

India is home to over one billion people, with approximately 83% following the Hindu religion. Hinduism features 16 major ceremonies throughout a person's life. One of the most significant is the naming ceremony, held 12 days after a baby is born. During this ritual, parents write the sacred word "Om" on the baby's tongue using a gold pen dipped in honey. A priest prepares a horoscope based on the stars and planets to suggest an auspicious name. Cultural etiquette varies globally. In China, it is impolite to leave chopsticks standing upright in rice. In many cultures, the 'host' is responsible for looking after guests and offering refills.
