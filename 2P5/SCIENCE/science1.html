Summary: Living Things in Their Environment

Ecosystems are defined by the interaction between biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) factors. Producers (plants) capture sunlight via photosynthesis to create energy. Consumers (animals) rely on producers or other consumers. Decomposers (fungi/bacteria) break down organic matter to recycle nutrients back into the soil. Food chains represent a single path of energy, while food webs show multiple interconnected paths. Symbiotic relationships define how organisms coexist: Mutualism (both benefit), Commensalism (one benefits, one unaffected), and Parasitism (one benefits, one harmed).

Memory Trick: "M-C-P Rule" -> Mutual (+,+), Commensal (+,0), Parasite (+,-). Always look for the 'Arrow of Energy' flow!

Thought Process: Identify the Producer first. Energy moves from food source to the feeder. Decomposers are the cleanup crew of the ecosystem.
